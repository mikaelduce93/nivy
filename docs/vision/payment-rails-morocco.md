## 1. Vision intended

The founder's stated north star for the second currency was *"ideally a
stablecoin but we can't"*. Crypto issuance is **not** legally available in
Morocco — Bank Al-Maghrib, the Autorité Marocaine du Marché des Capitaux and
the Office des Changes issued the joint warning of 2017-11-20 banning crypto
transactions, reaffirmed in 2018, and a draft framework (Bill 2024) is still
in consultation but has not entered into force. Issuing a token, even as a
"private game balance backed by DH", risks being requalified by BAM as either
unauthorised e-money or, worse, an unauthorised crypto-asset.

The vision therefore reduces to a **prepaid e-money / escrow** model:

1. The parent deposits dirhams via a regulated rail (CMI card acquiring,
   Mobile Money operator, ambassador cash collection, or — for international
   parents — Stripe).
2. The deposited DH lands in a Nivy-controlled bank account and is **held
   1:1** as escrow for the teen's benefit.
3. The teen sees a "coin" balance equal to the escrowed DH (or a fixed
   conversion of it). The coin is a database row, not a token, not a
   transferable instrument, not redeemable in cash by the teen — only spent
   inside Nivy's catalog (events, partner discounts, mystery boxes, profile
   customisation) or refunded to the parent on demand.
4. Compliance with Loi 103-12 (banking law), Loi 78-12 (BAM statute,
   payment-services chapter) and Loi 09-08 (data protection / parent KYC)
   must be designed in, not bolted on.

This means Nivy is, by default, a **prepaid wallet operator on top of a
licensed PSP**. Either Nivy itself becomes a licensed Établissement de
Paiement (EP) — a multi-year BAM process — or it partners with one (Cash Plus,
Wafacash, Damane Cash, M2T, Naps) and the partner is the "issuer of record"
of the e-money while Nivy is just the front-end UX.

## 2. Code state

### 2.1 Inventory of payment integrations

The repo wires four real-money rails plus one virtual rail:

- **Stripe** — `lib/stripe.ts:1-67` (lazy SDK init keyed on
  `STRIPE_SECRET_KEY`, `apiVersion: '2025-10-29.clover'`,
  `formatPriceToStripe(amountInDirhams)` multiplies by 100 to send centimes
  in MAD). Used by `app/api/payments/hybrid/route.ts:256-305` to create a
  `checkout.sessions.create` for `event_booking` items in MAD with
  metadata bundling `bookingId, userId, teenId, xpUsed, xpValue, type:'hybrid_payment'`
  and success/cancel URLs back to `/mes-reservations/<bookingId>`.
- **CMI (Centre Monétique Interbancaire)** — `lib/payments/cmi.ts:45-377`
  is a full hosted-form integration against `est3Dgate` (test endpoint
  `https://testpayment.cmi.co.ma/fim/est3Dgate`), `storetype: '3D_PAY_HOSTING'`,
  `trantype: 'PreAuth'`, `currency: '504'` (MAD ISO 4217), SHA-512 hash of
  `clientid|oid|amount|okUrl|failUrl|storetype|trantype|rnd|storeKey`. Has a
  full mock mode for dev (`createMockPayment`, l.293-332), 17 mapped response
  codes (l.337-360), and consumes envs `CMI_MERCHANT_ID`, `CMI_STORE_KEY`,
  `CMI_CLIENT_ID`, `CMI_API_ENDPOINT`. Routes:
  `app/api/payments/cmi/create/route.ts`, `.../initiate/route.ts`,
  `.../callback/route.ts` (browser redirect, marks booking paid, inserts into
  `payment_transactions`, sends Resend confirmation email — l.59-96), and
  `.../webhook/route.ts` (S2S, idempotent on `payment_status='paid'`, logs to
  `webhook_events` — l.45-103). Gated by feature flag `cmi_payment` read from
  `lib/features/flags` (`hybrid/route.ts:309`); `.env.example:157` defaults
  it to `false`.
- **Mobile Money (Orange Money / inwi Money / MT Cash)** —
  `lib/payments/mobile-money.ts:47-72` registers three operators with their
  USSD codes (`#150#`, `*120#`, `#111#`), env-driven merchant codes
  (`ORANGE_MONEY_*`, `INWI_MONEY_*`, `MT_CASH_*`). The `initiatePayment`
  flow generates an 8-digit code + payment ID, validates Moroccan phone
  format `0[67]\d{8}` (l.234-239), and returns USSD-walkthrough instructions
  the teen reads on screen (l.305-355). The actual operator API call is a
  **placeholder** that just logs the request (`callOperatorAPI`, l.276-300)
  — production verification (`verifyPayment`, l.182-209) returns
  `{verified:false, error:'Vérification manuelle requise'}`. Route entry
  point: `app/api/payments/mobile-money/initiate/route.ts`. Gated by feature
  flag `mobile_money_payment` (`hybrid/route.ts:353`).
- **Cash via ambassador** — `app/api/payments/cash/create/route.ts:1-90`
  accepts a `bookingId` + `ambassadorId`, validates the ambassador role,
  inserts a row in `payment_transactions` (`method:'cash_ambassador'`,
  `status:'pending'`) and a row in `cash_settlements`
  (`status:'received'`). The teen/parent then physically hands cash to the
  ambassador. There is no signature, photo of receipt, or settlement
  reconciliation flow — just two `INSERT`s.
- **XP (virtual)** — `lib/payments/xp-converter.ts:1-225` is the only
  internal currency rail wired into checkout. `XP_TO_DH_RATE = 0.10`
  (10 XP = 1 DH), `MIN_XP_FOR_PAYMENT = 50`, `PARENTAL_APPROVAL_THRESHOLD_XP = 1000`.
  Used by `hybrid/route.ts` to compute the hybrid split before delegating
  the cash leg to one of the three real rails above.

### 2.2 Parent top-up — the missing rail

`app/api/parent/topup/route.ts:1-175` is the route the founder vision
implicitly relies on (parent buys coins → escrow grows → teen spends). What
it actually does:

- Validates parent role, parent-teen link (`parent_teen_links` with
  `status='active'`), e-signature (`e_signatures.terms_accepted=true`).
- Reads body `{ parentId, teenId, packageId, coins, bonus, price }`.
- Tries to `SELECT total_coins FROM profiles WHERE id = teenId` (l.74-78)
  and then `UPDATE profiles SET total_coins = newTotal` (l.91-97). The
  column `profiles.total_coins` does not exist in the live schema — this
  flow silently 404s on every call. **No DH is collected at any point in
  this route**: `price` is body-passed but never sent to Stripe / CMI /
  Mobile Money. Coins (when the SELECT works on legacy fixtures) are
  credited as if the parent had already paid out-of-band. There is no
  PSP integration on the top-up side at all.

This is the central rails gap: the only legal "wallet top-up" entry point
is non-functional and not wired to a payment processor. CMI/Stripe/Mobile
Money are all wired only to *checkout* (paying for a single booking), not
to *funding* a balance.

### 2.3 The neutral fallback

`app/api/payments/process/route.ts:1-176` is the legacy formData processor.
It explicitly hard-codes `CARD_GATEWAY_AVAILABLE = false` (l.24), refuses
`paymentMethod='card'` with a 503 `PAYMENT_GATEWAY_UNAVAILABLE`, and for
`mobile_money|bank_transfer|ambassador` writes a `payment_status='pending'`
intention without claiming success. This is the only honest part of the
stack — it acknowledges the rails are not ready and does not lie with a
fake `paid`.

## 4. Legal landscape (Morocco)

- **Loi 103-12** (Code bancaire, 2014) and **Loi 78-12** (BAM statute) define
  the regulated activities: receiving public funds, issuing means of payment,
  issuing electronic money. Loi 103-12 art. 16 carves out the
  *Établissement de Paiement* (EP) status. Holding parent prepaid balances
  to be spent later — what Nivy does with "coins" — is **issuance of
  electronic money** under BAM Circular 6/W/2017 and requires either an EP
  licence (capital floor 3 M MAD for narrow scope, 6-10 M MAD for full
  scope) or a partnership with an existing EP as agent / distributor.
- **Office des Changes regulations** apply if the parent is a non-resident
  topping up a Moroccan teen's wallet, or vice-versa: Stripe USD/EUR flows
  to a Moroccan beneficiary involve foreign-exchange controls (Instruction
  Générale des Opérations de Change 2024 ed.).
- **Loi 09-08** (data protection, supervised by CNDP) requires registered
  treatment of parent identity data (CIN, phone, email) used for KYC.
  Combined with the upcoming **Loi 31-25** consultation on minor consent,
  this means parent-teen linkage and top-up audit trails are CNDP-declarable
  treatments.
- **Crypto** — joint Bank Al-Maghrib / AMMC / Office des Changes
  communiqué of 2017-11-20 prohibits crypto transactions; the 2024 Loi
  cadre on crypto-assets is in consultation but not enacted. Issuing a
  Nivy token, even as an "internal score backed by DH", risks being
  requalified by BAM as either unlicensed e-money or unlicensed
  crypto-asset issuance. The "stablecoin" framing is therefore non-viable
  and the founder's intuition to fall back to a pure prepaid model is
  correct.
- **Approved acquirers / PSPs** for retail card payments: CMI has the
  domestic monopoly on dirham-card acquiring under BAM authorisation
  (which is why `lib/payments/cmi.ts` exists). For e-money/wallet
  issuance the licensed players are M2T (Inwi-affiliated), Cash Plus,
  Wafacash, Damane Cash, Naps, Barid Cash, Maroc Telecommerce. Stripe
  is **not** licensed in Morocco; Stripe MAD payments route through a
  foreign acquirer and only work for international cards, which makes
  it a poor primary rail for a Morocco-first product.

## 5. Architectural gaps

1. **No escrow accounting model.** No table records "Nivy holds X DH on
   behalf of parent Y for teen Z, dated, with PSP reference". Coin balances
   in `user_coins` have no provable backing in any DH ledger.
2. **No PSP wired to top-up.** `app/api/parent/topup/route.ts` is the only
   place the founder's vision can land, and it has zero calls to Stripe,
   CMI, or Mobile Money. Real money never enters the system through the
   top-up flow today.
3. **`profiles.total_coins` does not exist** but is the column the top-up
   route reads/writes — broken on every call.
4. **`coin_transactions` write uses wrong columns** (`user_id`,
   `source_user_id` vs schema `teen_id`, `source_type`, `source_id`).
5. **`xp_to_dh_rate` divergence** between TS constant (`0.10`) and DB
   setting (`100`), with no code reading the DB row — silent dead config.
6. **No audit-trail tables exist live** (`payment_transactions`,
   `cash_settlements`, `webhook_events`, `payment_logs`,
   `parental_approvals`) — every "successful" CMI/Stripe/cash payment
   silently drops its audit-trail INSERT.
7. **No KYC of the parent before top-up.** Only an e-signature gate is
   enforced (`topup/route.ts:52-69`); no CIN/passport, no liveness check,
   no AML screening. BAM CDD (customer due diligence) requirements are
   not met for a regulated wallet.
8. **No refund mechanism for parent.** `refund_booking_xp` exists for XP
   (migration 021, l.120-157), but no DH-to-parent refund path exists in
   code or in DB.
9. **No transaction limits.** No per-day, per-month, per-transaction cap
   on top-ups or spends; BAM Circular 6/W/2017 imposes ceilings on
   anonymous and lightly-KYC'd e-money wallets.
10. **No reconciliation job.** Nothing reconciles CMI settlements (T+1 in
    Morocco) with Nivy's internal ledger, and no daily cash-flow report is
    generated for accounting / regulatory purposes.
11. **No "age-out" handling.** Nothing in code or schema describes what
    happens to a teen's coin balance when they turn 18.
12. **Mobile Money operator APIs are mocked.** Production verification
    returns "Vérification manuelle requise" — there is no real automated
    confirmation path; ops would have to manually mark each transfer.

## 6. Implementation options

### Option A — Pure escrow on a regulated bank account (Nivy as agent only)

Nivy opens a corporate dirham account with a tier-1 Moroccan bank, signs
an acquiring contract with CMI (already wired in code) and a Mobile Money
collection contract with one or two operators. Parent top-ups land in that
bank account; Nivy maintains an internal ledger (`parent_balance_ledger`,
`teen_balance_ledger`) backed by daily reconciliation against bank
statements. Coin balance = escrowed DH balance.

Pros: minimal regulatory burden (Nivy is just a tech provider over its
own corporate account, not an EP). Refunds go back to the source card via
CMI reversal. Audit trail is provable.

Cons: the parent funds are commingled with Nivy's operating funds in a
single corporate account — BAM may still requalify Nivy as an e-money
issuer above a balance threshold. Need a *trust account* / *compte de
cantonnement* with an explicit beneficial-ownership convention with the
bank, which most Moroccan banks do not standardly offer.

### Option B — Partner with a licensed e-money issuer (Cash Plus, M2T, Wafacash)

Nivy signs a white-label agreement with one of the licensed EPs and the
EP is the issuer of record of the wallet. Parent top-ups create wallet
balances at the EP; Nivy reads the balance via API. Coin balance =
EP-held balance.

Pros: regulatory burden moves to the EP. KYC, AML, ceilings, refunds
inherited from the EP's existing licensing. Cash Plus and Wafacash have
nationwide physical top-up networks (interesting for parents without a
card). M2T integrates with Inwi Money.

Cons: revenue share on every transaction (~1-2.5% to the EP). Less UX
control. Onboarding a new EP partner takes 6-12 months. Some EPs require
the wallet to be in the parent's name with their CIN — adds friction.

### Option C — DB-only "coins" with no real escrow (current minimal impl)

Coins are a pure database integer; parent never actually pays in DH for
them in this version. Used for engagement / UX testing only. Top-up shows
fake bonuses without invoking any PSP.

Pros: zero regulatory exposure (no real money). Fastest to ship.

Cons: doesn't deliver the vision — teens cannot actually buy real
products / discounts / event tickets with coins. Limits the entire economy
to XP. Founder's "prepaid wallet" intent is not met.

### Option D — Hybrid (Stripe for international parents, CMI for local cards, Mobile Money for unbanked, Cash for ambassador-network)

Keep all four rails wired into a single top-up entry point that branches
by `paymentMethod`. Stripe used only for non-MAD cards (parents living
abroad sending money home — large Moroccan diaspora). CMI used for MAD
cards. Mobile Money for parents without a card. Cash via ambassador for
the unbanked. Each rail credits the same internal escrow ledger.

Pros: coverage of all parent segments in the Moroccan market. Already
~70% wired in code (CMI complete, Stripe complete, Mobile Money skeleton,
Cash skeleton). Just needs the unification at top-up entry and the
escrow ledger.

Cons: four PSP relationships to maintain, four reconciliation flows,
four refund procedures. Compliance burden is the union of all four. Still
needs a position on Option A vs Option B for the underlying e-money
licence question.

**Recommendation matrix**: Option B + Option D combined is the most
realistic path — partner with one licensed EP for the wallet legal
wrapper (B), expose all four collection rails to the parent (D) as
funding methods into the EP-held wallet, and keep Stripe purely for
non-MAD cards.

## 7. Open questions

1. **Does Nivy hold an e-money licence or partner with one?** Today,
   neither — and yet the product UI shows a "coins" balance (`user_coins`
   table seeded with 100 coins for one teen). This is a regulatory exposure
   the moment a real DH-funded top-up happens. Founder must decide
   between Option A (Nivy as tech-only agent over its corporate bank
   account, narrow scope), Option B (partner with M2T / Cash Plus /
   Wafacash), or apply for an EP licence directly (multi-year).
2. **CMI contract status?** `CMI_MERCHANT_ID`, `CMI_STORE_KEY`,
   `CMI_CLIENT_ID` are referenced in `.env.example` and gated behind
   feature flag `cmi_payment` (default `false`). Has a CMI merchant
   contract been signed? What are the agreed MDR (merchant discount
   rate), settlement cycle (T+1?), refund window, and chargeback
   policy?
3. **Stripe Atlas vs Maroc Telecommerce for international cards?** Stripe
   is not licensed in MA; the SDK works but routing MAD-denominated
   transactions through Stripe needs a Stripe Atlas-style US/EU entity
   or a foreign acquirer. Is there a Nivy entity in Stripe-supported
   geography, or should Maroc Telecommerce be wired instead?
4. **Mobile Money operator API contracts?** Code references
   `ORANGE_MONEY_API_KEY`, `INWI_MONEY_API_KEY`, `MT_CASH_API_KEY` but
   the `callOperatorAPI` method just `console.log`s the request. No
   evidence any operator contract has been signed. Without it, mobile
   money is purely UX theatre with manual back-office verification.
5. **Refund policy for parent top-ups?** No code path exists. What's the
   commitment to the parent (full refund within 14 days? before any teen
   spend? minus a fee?), and how is it enforced when funds are partially
   spent on a partner discount?
6. **What happens to balances at the teen's 18th birthday?** Does the
   wallet auto-convert to a normal adult account, get cashed out to the
   parent, get frozen, or get donated? No code, no DB column, no founder
   doc addresses this. Has BAM consequence: a wallet held by a minor
   without parental oversight after 18 may need to be closed or
   re-KYC'd in the adult's name.
7. **Per-transaction / per-month limits?** BAM Circular 6/W/2017 imposes
   limits on lightly-KYC'd e-money wallets (typical: 200 DH single
   transaction, 5 000 DH monthly load, 20 000 DH annual for tier-1
   wallets). Nothing in code enforces this. Are these to be enforced by
   Nivy, by the partner PSP, or both?
8. **Cash settlement reconciliation with ambassadors?** The ambassador
   collects cash from the parent and the booking is marked `pending`,
   but there is no schedule, no commission, no signed receipt, no
   reconciliation cron, no cash-handling insurance. Operationally and
   legally fragile.
9. **AML / suspicious-transaction reporting?** No screening of parent
   identity against UAF (Unité de Traitement du Renseignement
   Financier) lists, no transaction-pattern monitoring. Required even
   under partner-PSP arrangements above modest thresholds.
10. **VAT treatment of coin top-ups?** Are coins prepaid vouchers (TVA
    payable on use, when the partner discount is consumed) or services
    (TVA at 20% on issuance)? Has this been validated with a tax
    advisor? The current `topup/route.ts` returns `success` without
    issuing a VAT-compliant invoice.
